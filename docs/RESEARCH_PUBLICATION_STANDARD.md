# FINORA research publication standard

Status date: 2026-07-13

## Purpose

FINORA's primary output is now research evidence. Each research-program report should
read like a small reproducible research paper, not a product update or engineering
note.

The goal is to publish evidence:

- What was tested
- Why it was tested
- What happened
- What failed
- What remains uncertain
- What should be tested next

## Required report structure

Each research publication should include:

1. Title
2. Abstract
3. Research question
4. Hypothesis
5. Dataset
6. Methodology
7. Experimental design
8. Results
9. Statistical tests
10. Failure analysis
11. Economic interpretation
12. Limitations
13. Decision
14. Next experiment

## Publication rules

- Do not present a result as alpha unless it survives robustness, statistical,
  economic, and paper-observation review.
- Report rejected and failed hypotheses as first-class research output.
- Separate evidence from interpretation.
- Preserve links to source datasets, campaign rows, reports, and decision artifacts.
- State the decision explicitly: reject, defer, continue research, paper observation,
  or retire.
- State what evidence would invalidate the hypothesis.
- Never convert a research publication into investment advice, a trade
  recommendation, or a commercial performance claim.

## Success metrics

FINORA should now be measured by:

- Research publications completed
- Hypotheses rejected
- Robustness studies completed
- Paper observations tracked
- Knowledge-base entries updated
- Failure conditions documented
- Reproducible evidence chains preserved

FINORA should not be measured by new models, dashboards, APIs, or architecture unless
research evidence proves they are necessary.
